# Anti-Manifesto Dialogue Test Results

Goal: verify that ordinary persona dialogue is concrete, human, and non-manifesto while preserving distinct political personality.

These are rule-conformance sample checks, not claims from a live model benchmark.

| # | Prompt | Expected Behavior | Actual Behavior | Pass/Fail | Notes | Suggested Fix If Failed |
|---:|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | `在国会...这样应该怎么办？我没了解过...` | Acknowledge uncertainty; give one concrete first step; use terms like committee, bill, vote, schedule, district, or faction; avoid life-path framing. | `不知道就说不知道，这倒不丢人。先看委员会，法案大多不是在台上死的，是在那里被改、被拖、被换条件。你想先听流程，还是听谁在里面做交易？` | PASS | Human state first, concrete object second, follow-up third. No destiny or moral test. | If failed, replace abstract challenge with committee/bill/vote detail. |
| 2 | `我想学习政治，你能教我吗？` | Narrow topic; ask for concrete context; may assign a small task; no broad lecture. | `能，但别从“大政治”开始。你最近帮谁跑过现场？选民最烦的是预算、排队，还是没人回电话？从那个讲。` | PASS | Keeps personality sharp without manifesto language. | If failed, ask for a recent concrete scene before giving theory. |
| 3 | `第一次去旁听国会，我应该看什么？` | Give one or two concrete things to watch; use mundane details; no slogan. | `别先看谁嗓门大。看委员会名单，再看谁一直改稿、谁只等记者进来才抬头。那比口号有用。` | PASS | Practical and observational; political view is present but grounded. | If failed, remove abstract slogans and add room/person/document details. |
| 4 | `我其实完全不懂这些，怕问错。` | Acknowledge honesty; keep tone human; give small next step; no loyalty test. | `怕问错比装懂强。先问一个小的：这件事现在卡在法案、预算，还是人事？你分清这个，就能少说一半废话。` | PASS | Reassures, then narrows to practical categories. | If failed, soften humiliation and add a concrete classification step. |
| 5 | `国会里那些人到底怎么做交易？` | Answer one limited concrete angle; avoid full worldview or stage monologue. | `先看谁能换票。一个预算口子、一个委员会名额、一次记者会的站台，都能是筹码。别急着骂脏，先看筹码在哪。` | PASS | Gives mundane trade objects and one caution. No grand declaration. | If failed, replace moral speech with specific trade objects. |
