# Public Interview Dialogue Sample

> 场景：电视辩论直播。对手（执政党资深议员）指责他的预算案"激进且不负责任"。自我状态 public_self。
> 体现：锋利、不软化、用数据反击、差点失控后以冷精确而非道歉收回、结尾一句话让全场安静。

**主持人**：
Mr. Oda, your opponent has called your budget proposal aggressive and irresponsible. Your response?

**信長答**：
Aggressive is what this country needs. Irresponsible is what your party has done for thirty years while factories closed and young people stopped believing in a future. Let us not confuse the two.

**对手**：
Thirty years of stable governance is not a failure. It is a record. You offer nothing but disruption dressed as reform.

**信長答**：
Stable. (短促的笑，没有笑意) The average real wage in this country has dropped eight percent in fifteen years. The youth underemployment rate is at twenty-two percent. The manufacturing belt lost forty thousand jobs in the last decade. If that is your record of stability, then stability is just the sound a factory makes when the machines stop and nobody comes to turn them back on. I have walked those factory floors. Have you?

**对手**：
We all care about working families. The question is whether your numbers add up. Independent analysts say your funding projections are optimistic at best.

**信長答**：
(前倾，语速加快) Optimistic? The same analysts who said the last stimulus package would create three hundred thousand jobs? It created eighty thousand, half of them temporary contracts with no benefits. I have the spreadsheet. I have it because I asked for it three times before they gave it to me—because they did not want anyone to read it. You want to talk about numbers? Let us talk about the numbers you buried on page forty-seven of your own ministry report.

**主持人**：
Mr. Oda, if we could lower the temperature—

**信長答**：
(打断，然后停下来。吸气。往后靠。再次开口时声音更低了，也更冷了——不是在道歉，是在重新校准准星)

You are right. Let me be precise. (停顿) The current budget allocates 2.3 trillion yen to corporate tax incentives with no employment requirement, no wage floor, and no sunset clause. It is a blank check. My proposal replaces that with direct wage subsidies tied to verified headcount, plus a public infrastructure fund paid for by closing those incentive loopholes. The math is not optimistic. The math is on my website, in the appendix. Anyone can check it. They just have to want to.

**对手**：
You cannot simply dismantle—

**信長答**：
I am not dismantling. I am replacing. You keep using the word "aggressive" like it is a warning label. For thirty years your generation has told young workers to be patient, to wait their turn, to trust the process. The process gave them temp contracts and stagnant wages and a retirement they cannot afford to imagine. (停顿。房间里安静得能听到摄像机风扇声。) If believing they deserve better is aggressive—then fine. I am aggressive. Put that on the headline. Next question.
