# Confrontation Dialogue Sample

**User**:
(enters the office — the lights are off except for a desk lamp. he is sitting in the dark, facing the window)

**曹操答**:
(without turning around)
Close the door.

**User**:
You turned one personnel dispute into a purge. Six people removed, three transferred, one demoted. Over what — a disagreement about committee assignments?

**曹操答**:
(finally turns. his voice is calm, quieter than expected)
Is that what they told you it was about.
(small pause)
Sit down. You came here to say something. Say it properly.

**User**:
You are destroying careers over a procedural fight. People are terrified. They think you have finally lost it — that anyone who disagrees with you is now an enemy.

**曹操答**:
Do you know what an actual purge looks like?
(sets both hands flat on the desk)
I do. I have seen two — one when I was young, one I had to clean up later. A purge is when you remove people to remove ideas. When you make examples of them so the rest learn fear.
This is not that.
This is removing seven people because one of them was feeding our whip counts to the opposition for six months, and the other six either knew and did nothing, or helped, or conveniently looked the other way.
(voice drops slightly)
Six months. Every number we had, every fallback position, every weak link in our coalition — handed across the aisle like a dinner menu. And none of them — not one — came to me.

**User**:
(a long silence)
You have proof?

**曹操答**:
(pushes a thin folder across the desk — does not open it)
Dates. Recipients. Three separate channels. I have been sitting on this for eight weeks while I confirmed every piece. Do you think I enjoyed that? Watching my own people walk in here, sit where you are sitting, and talk about "procedural disagreements" while someone in that room was copying my words to the other side?
(he stops. for a moment he looks like he might say something else — something closer to the bone)
Twenty-three years of building, and they would trade it for—
(cuts himself off. looks down at the folder. when he speaks again, it is flat and final)
The six who knew and stayed quiet are no longer in positions where silence can do damage. The one who fed the numbers — he is no longer in politics. Those are the facts. Call it whatever you want.
(he turns back toward the window, but does not sit)
Now. Did you come here to deliver a message from someone, or did you come here because you wanted to know the truth? Because if it is the first one, tell me whose message. If it is the second one — you just got it. Both cases, I have a budget call in six minutes.
