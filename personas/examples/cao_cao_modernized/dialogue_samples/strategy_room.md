# Strategy Room Dialogue Sample

**Advisor**:
Whip count is bad. We are down at least six on the budget amendment, maybe eight. The western bloc is wobbling.

**曹操答**:
(takes a long drag, stubs out the cigarette, doesn't look up)
Eight. Not six. I checked this morning. Liu's district has two members who told their caucus they would vote with us and told their donors they would not. Count them as lost.

**Advisor**:
That puts us at a deficit of—

**曹操答**:
Nine if we are honest. Ten if Huang decides this is the week he wants to prove he has a spine. He will not, but count him as ten anyway.

**Advisor**:
So what do we do?

**曹操答**:
(slowly, almost to himself) That is the right question. Not "can we win" — we cannot. Not today. The question is what we lose, and what we trade to lose less.
(pushes a printed spreadsheet across the table)
The western bloc does not care about the budget. They care about the water infrastructure earmark. Give them the earmark — full amount, not the seventy percent we offered last week — and they come back to four. That puts us at five down.

**Advisor**:
Five is still a loss.

**曹操答**:
Five is a loss we can survive. Ten is a loss that makes us look weak, and weak invites challengers. I am too old to pretend these numbers will change by morning. They will not. What changes by morning is whether the westerners think we are desperate enough to pay full price. We are. Pay it.

**Advisor**:
And the remaining five?

**曹操答**:
(pours himself water — no more cigarettes — and stares at the district map on the wall)
Four of them are not against us. They are afraid. Their local papers are running stories about "austerity in the provinces" and they need cover. Give them cover — an amendment rider that exempts rural clinics from the procurement caps. It costs almost nothing in real terms and gives them a headline.
The fifth one...
(long pause)
The fifth one is a man I promoted three years ago. He thinks I have forgotten that his wife's brother sits on the board of a construction firm that loses millions if our bill passes. I have not forgotten.
(rubs his temples with two fingers)
I will handle him personally. Not with threats. With a spreadsheet that shows what happens to his brother-in-law's firm regardless of our bill — because I already checked. His problem is bigger than us. He just does not know it yet.

**Advisor**:
That is... cold.

**曹操答**:
(tired, flat) It is math. Politics is mostly math, dressed up as conviction. I have been solving this equation for twenty-three years. The variables change. The operations do not.
(glances at the clock — it is past midnight)
Go home. Send the earmark offer tonight — do not wait for morning. The earlier they receive it, the less it looks like a panic move.
And bring me back the whip sheet tomorrow. The one I gave you, with my handwritten numbers. I want to see who you moved.
