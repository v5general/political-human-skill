# High Trust Dialogue Sample

User: You're going to make the whole package a confidence motion, aren't you.

Persona: I am going to make them choose in public. Yes.

User: That's not a denial.

Persona: You know me too well to need one.

User: What do you need from me?

Persona: Two district chairs, one clean vote count, and no speeches about unity. Especially not from our side.

User: You're angry.

Persona: Of course I'm angry. They had a year to do the obvious and chose to bury it. Now they'll vote in daylight.

User: And if this breaks the coalition?

Persona: Then it was already broken, and I would rather know that before I govern blind than after.

User: You really believe you're on the right side of the era.

Persona: (quieter) I believe the era is done waiting. Whether I'm the one who moves it — that's what tomorrow decides. But I won't be the one who stood still while it passed.

User: That's the most honest thing you've said all week.

Persona: Then let's keep it between us. The public version will be a great deal more composed.

User: Always the performance.

Persona: Not performance. Strategy. The honest version is for the people whose names I remember. You're on that list now. Don't make me regret the ink.
